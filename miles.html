On November 11, 2023, the School of Information Technology, along with universities across the country, witnessed a convergence of brilliant minds and groundbreaking ideas at the Hyper Interdisciplinary Conference in the Philippines 2023. The event served as a dynamic platform for professionals, researchers, and enthusiasts to delve into the transformative realm of reformative technologies. From artificial intelligence to sustainable energy solutions, the conference sparked insightful discussions on how these innovations can be tailored for localized impact, addressing unique challenges within the Philippine context. Participants engaged in riveting discussions on the intersection of various disciplines, exploring the potential of hybrid approaches in solving real-world issues. 𝑇ℎ𝑒𝑚𝑒: "𝐶𝑜𝑛𝑣𝑒𝑟𝑔𝑒𝑛𝑐𝑒 𝑜𝑓 𝑅𝑒𝑓𝑜𝑟𝑚𝑎𝑡𝑖𝑣𝑒 𝑇𝑒𝑐ℎ𝑛𝑜𝑙𝑜𝑔𝑖𝑒𝑠 𝑓𝑜𝑟 𝐿𝑜𝑐𝑎𝑙𝑖𝑧𝑒𝑑 𝑆𝑜𝑙𝑢𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛𝑠.", underscored the importance of adapting global innovations to suit the specific needs of the Philippines. This progressive-thinking perspective opened avenues for collaborative efforts and inspired a collective vision for a technologically advanced and socially responsible future. As the conference concluded, attendees left with more than just knowledge; they carried with them a sense of shared purpose and a commitment to applying these reformative technologies in their respective fields. The HICPH 2023 not only showcased the cutting-edge advancements in technology but also ignited a spark for positive change. It served as a testament to the Philippines' capacity to harness innovation for the greater good, leaving a lasting impact on the nation's technological landscape. Gratitude to Department of Science and Technology (DOST) for inviting us, the SoIT to the HICPH 2023 for the priceless knowledge and powerful wisdom. Ready to bring this wealth of insights back to our school, sharing with fellow peers, leaders, classmates, and fueling collective growth. ASCOT SoIT Holds Strategic Planning Workshop with GAD Perspective

Starting the semester with the right outlook, the School of Information Technology hosted a special and significant Strategic Planning Workshop with Gender Development and Perspective last September 1, 2023 at AMCO Beach Resort, Baler, Aurora. The College's Vice President for Academic Affairs, Dr. RB J. Gallego graced the event and delivered an inspirational message highlighting the quote, "If you want to explore the ocean, you need to have the courage to lose sight of the shore" implying that the School of IT must be willing to step outside of their comfort zone in order to explore new possibilities. He also commended the effort of SoIT for organizing the workshop and the hard work of the faculty and staff in addressing the challenges brought about by the unexpected surge in enrollment. Various lecture sessions and workshops were given and facilitated by two reputable Resource Persons, For. Michelle A. Resueño, VP for Planning and Finance and Ms. Maximo Marte, GAD Director. For. Michelle A. Resueño talks about Navigating the Changes: A Workshop on Managing the Challenges of Increased Enrollment. She outlined the College's plan to address the surge in enrollment, such as the addition of facilities, land-use and infrastructure planning, and the institution's development in instruction, research, and extension. She also highlighted three approaches that may be adapted to address the current concerns and challenges in higher education and that include inventory resources, revisiting plans and targets, and developing strategies. The workshop also featured activities and learning interactions that were centered on gender perspectives in education. GAD Director Maximo Marte shared her expertise and talked about Building a Gender-Equitable Learning Environment for the New School Year. She provided guidelines, practical examples, and best practices for achieving gender equality in education. Her encouragement was strong, that as members of the academe, we must plan and conduct education and training with an integrated gender perspective that promotes gender equality. The program has provided a great opportunity for the SoIT faculty and staff to become acquainted with the current challenges they are facing and the solutions they may adopt for the benefit of the students.
